## Decisions that stuck

- **Interactive only.** Every claim is human-initiated; autonomous runs stay in schedules. Kept the skill surface small (no companion agent).
- **Stateless, tracker-as-truth.** No local task cache; every op reads live → transitions → writes back. Made multi-user safe by construction.
- **Collapse over refuse** for unmapped optional stages (owner call at ROS-151 plan time): `done` bridges an unmapped Review; `submit`/`cancel` degrade to guided no-ops; `claimed` reconstructs from the assignment overlay. Minimal boards (ready/active/done) get the full verb surface with honest notes.
- **Strict concurrency guard dropped** (owner call): `claim` = self-assign + status; the original read-back-guard acceptance was superseded — human-initiated claims on operator boards don't need CAS.
- **`block` posts the reason as a comment BEFORE the status write** — a failed status write never loses the reason. Cost: `block` is not idempotent (retry duplicates the comment); documented.
- **Digest in the CLI, not skill prose** (ROS-152): one tested renderer; the skill relays precomputed `groups`/`attention` JSON.

## What surprised us

- **Node `--experimental-strip-types` rejects TS parameter properties** while tsc/tsdown accept them — typecheck+build pass but the test runner fails to load the file. Declare fields explicitly.
- **Assigned-Ready is two different things** depending on the board: with Claimed mapped it derives `ready` (not claimed); without, the overlay kicks in. The status digest buckets both under "Claimed / assigned" with honest per-row `canonical` to keep the partition total.
- **Notion API 2026-03-11**: pages are trashed with `in_trash`, not `archived`; data-source parents replace database parents in page creation.
- Codex review gates earned their keep: ROS-149 6→9 (live-restore destructiveness), ROS-151 5→7→8 (three fix rounds), ROS-152 7→9 (skill prompt-injection hardening was a genuine catch).

## What we'd do differently

- Budget the Codex fix rounds into estimates up front (2–4 rounds on anything touching live boards or shipped skills).
- The EXPERT/scaffold list-drift class of problem (found during ROS-144 recon) suggests: when three artifacts must stay in sync, either generate them from one source or ship the checklist that syncs them — decided per ROS-144/129 follow-ups.
