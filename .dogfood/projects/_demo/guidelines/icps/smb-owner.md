# ICP: SMB Owner

## Who they are
- Owner-operator of a small-to-mid business (10–100 employees)
- Industries: services (consulting, agencies), light manufacturing, retail
- Region: US/Canada/UK initially
- Personal: 35–55, runs the business themselves, often without a CFO

## What they care about
- Time saved (their time + their bookkeeper's)
- Accuracy (they get audited; mistakes are expensive)
- Cost-effectiveness (every dollar in software is a dollar not in payroll)

## What they don't care about (or actively dislike)
- "Scalability" — they're not trying to be a unicorn
- Slick demos with imaginary use cases
- Long sales cycles or "talk to sales" gates

## Buying signals
- Posting hiring for a bookkeeper or controller
- Recently changed accounting software
- Mentions of "growing pains" in financial ops

## Anti-signals
- Already enterprise-tier (they have a CFO and dedicated finance team)
- Pre-revenue / pre-product (they're not yet generating books-worthy activity)

## Where to find them
- LinkedIn: founders + owners with 10–100 employee count
- Industry communities (e.g., bookkeeping/accounting subreddits, IndieHackers)
- Local chamber of commerce events
